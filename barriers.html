The value isn't enough. And the product is scary.

Most merchants who see Wix Checking don't proceed. Not because of friction — because the offer doesn't justify the perceived risk. We need to add real value AND dissolve real fears.

Incentives

Make the Offer Irresistible

  • Balance rewards (~1.5% APY) — competitive with Shopify Balance. Earn on every dollar held.
  • Instant access to earnings — vs. 2-3 day wait for external payouts. Speed sells.
  • Free Wix Premium trial — or reduced subscription fees for Wix Balance users.

Even with great incentives, four fears remain. Value alone won't get us there.

Fear 1

"It's a Bank Account"

Merchants hear "checking account" and think bank. They don't want another bank — they want to get paid.
The Fix
  • Rebrand to "Wix Balance" — drop the banking language. A/B test naming impact on conversion.
  • Position as a payout wallet — not a financial product. Just a better way to receive and manage earnings.
Fear 2

"My Money Gets Stuck"

The #1 rational objection. Merchants fear losing control of cash flow if money enters a Wix account.
The Fix
  • Auto-payout to external bank by default. Money flows through — arrives, transfers out automatically. No lock-in.
  • Merchants keep the speed advantage — instant access during the day, auto-transfer at their pace.
Fear 3

"I Don't Want a Debit Card"

The debit card signals commitment to a new financial product. It feels permanent.
The Fix
  • Virtual-only by default — no physical card unless explicitly requested.
  • De-emphasize the card — it's a bonus feature, not a core part of Wix Balance.
Fear 4

"What Even Is This Product?"

Some merchants are simply afraid of the unknown. They don't understand what Wix Balance is or how it changes their setup.
The Fix
  • Shadow / preview mode — let merchants see their balance dashboard without committing. Build familiarity before asking for a decision.
  • Progressive education — show how earnings flow, what changes, and what stays the same.
All Fears Dissolved

Now we can change the default.

It's not called "checking." Money auto-flows out. No card commitment. They've previewed it. There is no harm in the new experience.
