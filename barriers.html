Where We Lose Them

75K eligible merchants. 1.7K converted. Four drops carve away 97.7%. Each needs a different strategy.

Reach — Drops 1 & 2
39%+ 72%

Never see it, or aren't pulled in

29K eligible merchants haven't encountered the product. Of those who have, 72% aren't engaged enough to explore further. Entry points aren't visible or contextual enough.

Reach — What We Do

Get Seen, In Context

  • Contextual entry points — surface on products where instant fund availability matters (stores, bookings, restaurants).
  • VIP customer outreach — targeted campaigns for high-value merchants with the most to gain.
  • Payouts page takeover — meet merchants where they already manage money.
  • Active choice modal — force a clear decision: new experience or legacy payouts. No more ignoring.
Convince — Drop 3
77%

Saw the pitch, said no

77% who reach the welcome screen don't proceed. They saw the value proposition — it wasn't compelling enough, or the product itself felt unfamiliar and risky.

This is the hardest drop. Two root causes: the value isn't enough, and the product triggers fear.

Convince — Add Value

Make the Offer Worth It

  • Balance rewards (~1.5% APY) — earn on every dollar held. Competitive with Shopify Balance.
  • Free Wix Premium trial — or reduced subscription fees for adopters. Tangible, immediate value.
  • Emphasize instant access — earnings available immediately vs. 2-3 day external payout wait. Already live, under-communicated.

Value gets attention. But four fears still block the "yes."

Fear 1

"It's a Bank Account"

Merchants hear "checking account" and think bank. They don't want another bank — they want to get paid.

The Fix
  • Rebrand to "Wix Balance" — drop the banking language. A/B test naming impact on conversion.
  • Position as a payout wallet — not a bank account. Just a better way to receive earnings.

Shopify and Square both position similar products as "Balance" rather than bank accounts — the naming shift has industry precedent.

Fear 2

"My Money Gets Stuck"

The #1 rational objection. Merchants fear losing control of cash flow if money enters a Wix account.

The Fix
  • Auto-payout to external bank by default. Money flows through — arrives, transfers out automatically. No lock-in.
  • Merchants keep the speed advantage — instant access during the day, auto-transfer at their pace.
Fear 3

"I Don't Want a Debit Card"

The debit card signals commitment to a new financial product. It feels permanent and unwanted.

The Fix
  • Virtual-only by default — no physical card unless explicitly requested.
  • De-emphasize the card — it's a bonus, not part of the core experience.
Fear 4

"I Don't Know What This Is"

Some merchants are simply wary of the unknown. They don't understand how it changes their setup or what they're signing up for.

The Fix
  • Shadow / preview mode — see the balance dashboard without committing. Build familiarity before asking for a decision.
  • Progressive education — show exactly how earnings flow, what changes, and what stays the same.
Activate — Drop 4
44%

Decided yes, didn't finish

44% who click "Open Account" drop during execution. Too many screens, too much process. They wanted it — we lost them at the door.

Activate — What We Do

Make It Effortless

  • One-click onboarding — account opens in one tap. Security steps deferred to post-activation.
  • Virtual card by default — no physical card commitment upfront.
  • Deferred onboarding — complete profile gradually, not all at once.

Reach. Convince. Activate.

But one barrier remains that no feature can solve: familiarity bias. "My setup works fine. Why change?"

The answer isn't another feature. It's making this the default — once there's nothing left to fear.
