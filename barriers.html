Four walls between us and 20K.

Each barrier is a rational response from merchants. We can't argue them away — we need to dissolve them with product changes.

Barrier 1

"It's a Bank Account"

Merchants hear "checking account" and think bank. They don't want another bank — they want to get paid.
The Fix
  • Rebrand to "Wix Balance" — drop the banking language entirely. A/B test naming impact on conversion.
  • Shadow mode — let merchants preview their balance dashboard without opening an account. Build desire before commitment. In development
  • Reframe as a payout wallet — not a financial product, just a better way to get paid.
Barrier 2

"My Money Gets Stuck"

The #1 rational objection. Merchants fear losing control of their cash flow if money enters a Wix account.
The Fix
  • Auto-payout to external bank by default. Money flows through — arrives in Wix Balance, transfers out automatically.
  • Same-day payouts — merchants get paid faster (vs. 2-3 day for external). The carrot they didn't know they wanted.
  • Transparent controls — clear dashboard showing every money movement. No surprises, no lock-in.
Barrier 3

"I Don't Want a Debit Card"

The debit card feels like a commitment to a new financial product. It signals permanence.
The Fix
  • Virtual-only by default — no physical card unless the merchant requests one.
  • De-emphasize the card — position it as a bonus feature, not a core requirement of Wix Balance.
  • One-click activation — remove the multi-step ceremony. Account opens in one tap, card is optional.
Barrier 4

"My Setup Works Fine"

This isn't a product problem — it's inertia. And inertia only breaks with a forcing function.
The Fix
  • 30-day risk-free trial — "Try Wix Balance. Not for you? Switch back in one click." Removes the permanence fear.
  • Same-day payout incentive — make the benefit tangible and immediate. Speed sells.
  • Change the default — once barriers 1-3 are solved, the default switch becomes nearly invisible. Auto-payout means merchants barely notice.
All Barriers

Dissolve the fear.
Then change the default.

When it's not called "checking," money auto-flows out, there's no card commitment, and there's a risk-free trial — changing the default isn't a controversial leap. It's barely noticeable.

The Key Unlock

Auto-payout is the skeleton key.

Solves Barrier 2 directly — merchants' money flows to their bank automatically. No lock-in.
Makes the default change safe — if money auto-flows out, switching merchants to Wix Balance is invisible. Stage 4 becomes a non-event.
Gives management confidence — "We're not trapping anyone's money. We're giving them faster payouts with an automatic bridge to their bank."
Preserves the business value — Wix still holds daily balances. Merchants still get same-day speed. Everyone wins.
03 - The Combined Strategy
Two Tracks. One Goal.
Dissolving barriers increases conversion rate. Escalation increases volume. Together: multiplicative.
Track A: Dissolve
Change What's Behind the Door
  • Wix Balance rebrand — A/B test naming across all touchpoints
  • Auto-payout default — money flows to external bank automatically
  • Shadow / demo mode — preview balance without commitment
  • Virtual card default — debit card is a bonus, not a requirement
  • 30-day risk-free trial — "Not for you? Switch back in one click"
  • Same-day payout emphasis — the tangible carrot merchants actually feel
Track B: Escalate
Drive Adoption at Scale
  • Continuous Stage 0 — ongoing optimization, value prop, demo mode
  • April Stage 1 — one-click onboarding, remove wizard friction
  • April Stage 2 — active choice modal, force a decision
  • June Stage 3 — pending payouts, escalate for holdouts
  • When safe Stage 4 — default change (unlocked by Track A)
Track A makes Track B safe. Without dissolved barriers, Stage 4 is a fight. With them, it's a formality. Auto-payout is the bridge between "controversial" and "obvious."
